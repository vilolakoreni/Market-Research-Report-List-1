<p><p>The wood veneers market comprises various types tailored for specific applications. Raw veneer is thin slices of wood, offering natural aesthetics. Paper-backed veneer provides flexibility and ease of installation. Phenolic-backed veneer enhances durability and moisture resistance, making it ideal for demanding environments. Laid-up veneer features multiple layers for added strength and stability. Reconstituted veneer uses engineered wood fibers to mimic natural wood patterns, promoting sustainability and uniformity. Each type serves distinct needs in furniture, cabinetry, and interior design.</p></p>
